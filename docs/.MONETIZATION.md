## Verified Attention Protocol (VAP)## Ecosystem Architecture & Commercialization Strategy: Open Core Model
Based on the project's guiding principles—particularly infrastructure before applications, interoperability, and scientific validation—this Open Core model structurally distinguishes between the foundational protocol (which must be open to ensure trust) and high-scale operational or specialized modules (which provide enterprise-grade value).
------------------------------
## 1. The "Free" Core (100% Open Source)
To maximize developer adoption and establish the protocol as a global Internet standard, these components must be free and independently auditable.

* VAP Specification: The normative rules, evidence models, and state machines are the property of the community and should eventually transition to a neutral foundation (e.g., IETF or W3C).
* Reference SDKs: Browser, mobile, and desktop SDKs must be open-source to ensure Privacy by Design and allow developers to verify that no covert surveillance or fingerprinting is occurring.
* VAE Reference Implementation (Basic): The core engine that processes session-scoped evidence and generates Proofs of Attention should be open-source to prove feasibility and establish a quality baseline.
* Standard Intelligence Models: Baseline AI models for behavioral modeling (e.g., standard reading paths and scroll rhythms) should be included to provide a deterministic, verifiable process.
* Conformance Test Suite: Automated tools to verify that third-party implementations are completely VAP-compatible.

[Open Source Core] ──> [VAP Specification] + [Reference SDKs] ──> Generates Basic Proofs

------------------------------
## 2. Premium Enterprise Modules (Paid/Gated)
These modules offer advanced performance, security, or specialized business logic that goes beyond the basic requirements of the protocol.

* Advanced Fraud Intelligence: While basic bot detection is in the core, advanced "Fraud Intelligence" that defends against AI-generated interaction patterns, Sybil attacks, and specialized emulator farms can be a premium offering.
* High-Scale Operational Infrastructure: A managed, cloud-native version of the VAE capable of handling billions of events per day with sub-second latency, featuring Kubernetes-based horizontally scaled orchestration and GPU-backed model inference.
* Reward Engine Pro: The basic Reward Engine recommends rewards, but an enterprise tier could include advanced campaign management, dynamic pricing strategies, and budget allocation tools for complex advertiser needs.
* Enterprise Analytics & Observability: Specialized dashboards tracking fraud rate trends and confidence distributions, tailored for corporate compliance or large-scale advertising audits.
* Dedicated Security & Compliance: Features like custom data retention policies, S3-style cold-evidence persistence, and dedicated secrets management for highly regulated industries like healthcare or finance.

[Enterprise Tier] ──> [High-Scale VAE Cloud] + [Advanced AI Fraud Engine] ──> Processes Billions of Events

* Trust through Openness: By keeping the SDKs and Protocol open, you solve the "information asymmetry" where publishers and advertisers currently have to trust platform-specific metrics blindly.
* Preventing Vendor Lock-in: Open-sourcing the core implementation ensures the infrastructure remains application-neutral, preventing the vendor lock-in that would be antithetical to the mission.
* Monetizing Scale and Edge Cases: Enterprises will pay for the operational excellence (handling massive traffic) and the ongoing arms race of high-end fraud detection, while the broader web adopts the free standard for basic verification.

## Apache License 2.0 (Client & Specifications)

* Target Components: Reference SDKs (Browser, Mobile), VAP Specification documents, Conformance Test Suites.
* Strategic Purpose: Permissive licensing ensures that any platform (including closed-source apps or proprietary web browsers) can integrate your SDKs without legal fear of inheriting a copyleft license. This removes all barriers to client-side adoption.

## GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPLv3) (Core Validation Engine)

* Target Components: VAE Reference Implementation (Basic), Standard Intelligence Models.
* Strategic Purpose: The AGPLv3 closes the "SaaS loophole". If a competitor or major cloud provider takes your open-source core validation engine, modifies it, and hosts it as a cloud service, they must open-source their modifications. This forces cloud giants to contribute back or pay for a commercial license.

## Commercial License (Enterprise Modules)

* Target Components: Advanced Fraud Intelligence, High-Scale Operational Infrastructure, Reward Engine Pro.
* Strategic Purpose: Grants enterprises the right to embed and run proprietary, high-throughput modules alongside the core engine without AGPL obligations, complete with service-level agreements (SLAs).

## Hook Point 1: The Behavior Stream Interceptor (/v1/evidence/process)

* Free Core Action: The SDK streams session-scoped evidence (scroll distances, timing vectors, focus shifts) to the Basic VAE. The Core performs data normalization and basic structural integrity checks (e.g., verifying cryptographic signatures of the payload).
* Premium Extension Injection: A plugin hook pauses the processing loop and forwards the normalized data structure to the Advanced Fraud Intelligence module. The premium engine evaluates the stream against real-time network-wide bot fingerprints and adversarial AI interaction models before returning a risk penalty score.

## Hook Point 2: The Score Resolver & Confidence Estimator (/v1/proof/generate)

* Free Core Action: Combines standard behavioral calculations to verify basic attention milestones, outputting a deterministic, baseline confidence value.
* Premium Extension Injection: The High-Scale Operational Infrastructure layer replaces the default local scoring database with a distributed, GPU-backed inference pipeline. This allows the system to process multi-dimensional weights at scale across millions of concurrent sessions, appending advanced risk metadata directly into the final Proof of Attention payload.

## Hook Point 3: The Antecedent State Handler (/v1/rewards/evaluate)

* Free Core Action: Exposes a webhook that notifies external systems when a Proof of Attention is successfully minted.
* Premium Extension Injection: The Reward Engine Pro consumes this hook directly. Instead of a simple pass-through notification, it ingests the proof into an enterprise-grade campaign budget processor, recalculating dynamic token payouts or fiat ad impressions based on fluctuating, client-defined demand curves.
